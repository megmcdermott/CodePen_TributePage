Georgia O'Keeffe Tribute Page

Georgia O'Keeffe

The mother of American Modernism

Timeline of O'Keeffe's life:

  • 1887 - Born in Sun Prairie, Wisconsin
  • 1905 - Graduates from Chatham Episcopal Institute in Virginia after moving there with her family to accomodate her father's new business making rusticated cast concrete blocks
  • 1905-1906 - Studies and ranked top of her class at the School of the Art Institute in Chicago
  • 1907 - Attends the Art Students League in New York City and won the William Merritt Chase still-life prize for her oil painting "Dead Rabbit with Copper Pot" a year later
  • 1912-14 - Teaches art in public schools in Amarillo in the Texas panhandle
  • 1915 - Taught at Columbia College in South Carolina where she completed a series of innovative charcoal abstractions
  • 1917 - Teaches and heads the art department at Texas State Normal College and painted one of her first works titled "The Flag" after her brother Alexis was shipped out to Europe for World War I
  • 1918 - Becomes sick during the 1918 flu pandemic after obtaining an art studio from photographer Alfred Stieglitz
  • 1922 - Begins creating simplified images of natural things like leaves, rocks, and flowers
  • 1924 - Marries Stieglitz after his divorce to his wife is finalized - 6 years after their affair began
  • 1925 - Begins creating a series of paintings of NYC skyscrapers and city skylines after moving into a 30th floor apartment in the Shelton Hotel
  • 1929 - Travels to Taos, New Mexico with her friend Rebecca Strand and completes her famous oil painting "The Lawrence Tree", as well as several other desert-based works
  • 1939 - Receives a painting request from the Dole pineapple company and travels to Hawaii for inspiration
  • 1946 - Begins incorporating the architectural forms of her Abiquiu house - purchased in 1945 - into her works, such as "Ladder to the Moon"
  • 1960 - Worcester Art Museum holds a retrospective on her work
  • 1972 - Loses most of her eyesight due to macular degeneration
  • 1973 - Hires John Bruce "Juan" as a live-in assistant and caretaker
  • 1984 - Moves to Santa Fe, New Mexico
  • 1986 - Dies at the age of 98
“I realized that I had things in my head not like what I had been taught – not like what I had seen – shapes and ideas so familiar to me that it hadn’t occurred to me to put them down. I decided to stop painting, to put away everything I had done, and to start to say the things that were my own.”
- Georgia O'Keeffe
